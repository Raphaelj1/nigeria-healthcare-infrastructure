Nigeria Healthcare Infrastructure Map

Explore comprehensive healthcare data with our interactive map, designed to drive better decision-making and resource allocation.

Mapping Nigeria’s Healthcare: Addressing Gaps, Unlocking Opportunities

Our team’s focus area for the 2024 Digital Nigeria Innovation Challenge is healthcare infrastructure. With a rapidly growing population and varying access to healthcare services across Nigeria, we aim to provide a comprehensive view of healthcare infrastructure to support data-driven decisions in addressing gaps and promoting equitable access to healthcare for all Nigerians.

The Story Behind the Data

Healthcare is an essential pillar of human development, and ensuring access to quality care is crucial for Nigeria’s future prosperity. However, our map reveals stark disparities across the nation. Using reliable, up-to-date data, we have mapped all healthcare facilities across Nigeria’s 36 states, offering insights into facility functionality and regional distribution.

Data Sources

To ensure the accuracy of our healthcare data, we sourced our information from the Nigeria Federal Ministry of Health, which provides detailed reports on healthcare facilities across the country. Population estimate and projection data which is critical to understanding the infrastructure needs in different regions, was gathered from the National Population Commission of Nigeria and the National Bureau of Statistics

Key Indicators for State Layer

  • Estimated Population: This figure indicates the total number of residents, providing insight into the community size and demographics.

  • Population Density (per km²):This metric reveals the number of individuals living in each square kilometre, highlighting population concentration and potential impacts on local resources and services.

  • Geopolitical Region:The state is situated in a specific region of Nigeria, which is essential for understanding its political, cultural, and socioeconomic context.

  • Total Facilities:This represents the overall number of healthcare facilities, offering an overview of the healthcare infrastructure and accessibility for residents.

  • Functional Facilities:This number indicates how many healthcare facilities are fully operational and capable of providing medical services, reflecting the effectiveness of the healthcare system.

  • Partially Functional Facilities:This metric highlights the number of facilities that offer limited services, pointing to areas that may require improvements or additional resources.

  • Non-functional Facilities:This figure shows the number of healthcare facilities that are completely inactive, raising concerns about the availability of healthcare services.

  • Facilities with Unknown Status:This reflects the number of facilities whose operational status is not known, complicating health service planning and resource allocation efforts.

One of the key features of the State layer on our map is the integration of population density data for each state, which helps illustrate the relationship between population concentration and healthcare availability. For each state, our map highlights:

  • The total number of healthcare facilities,

  • The number of functional, partially functional, and non-functional facilities,

  • The number of facilities with an unknown status

This combination of indicators offers a holistic view of healthcare demand. For example, a state with a high population density but a low number of functional facilities would face significant challenges in meeting its population’s healthcare needs, while states with a greater percentage of unknown or non-functional facilities may require immediate intervention to improve healthcare access.

Exploring the Local Government Layer: Mapping Boundaries and Facilities

In addition to state-level data, our map features an interactive local government layer, allowing users to explore healthcare infrastructure within each of Nigeria’s 774 local government areas (LGAs). When users select the local government layer, they can:

  • View the name and boundaries of the LGA,

  • See the healthcare facilities located within that LGA by clicking the healthcare layer to add them to the map.

This provides users with a visual representation of where healthcare facilities are located within each local government boundary. This helps identify geographic areas that are well-served and those that may need additional attention or resources.

Exploring the Geopolitical Region Layer: Mapping Facilities by Region

Our map features an interactive geopolitical region layer, which groups healthcare facilities based on Nigeria's six geopolitical regions: North East, North West, North Central, South East, South West, and South South. When users click on a geopolitical region, they can:

  • View all healthcare facilities located within that specific region,

  • Understand how facilities are distributed across the different states within the region.

This layer allows users to identify regions where healthcare resources are concentrated and those that may be underserved. For example, users can quickly see the number of healthcare facilities available in a region and assess if they align with the region's population needs.

Missing or Underdeveloped Infrastructure in Adamawa State as an example.

  • Healthcare Facilities: Adamawa State has 1,575 total healthcare facilities, with 865 categorised as having an unknown functional status. This lack of information highlights a significant gap in understanding the current state of healthcare infrastructure. The unknown facilities may include both non-operational facilities and those that are simply not documented well, which complicates planning and resource allocation.

  • Optional Clarity: The high number of unknown facilities indicates a need for thorough assessments to determine which facilities are functional and capable of providing services. This lack of clarity makes it challenging for decision-makers to understand where to invest resources and improvements.

  • Access to Care: Many rural areas may still lack adequate healthcare access due to an insufficient number of operational facilities. With many facilities being in an unknown status, it’s unclear whether these areas are served by nearby healthcare centres, thus underscoring the need for further infrastructure development in remote communities.

  • Specialised Services: There is likely a shortfall in specialised healthcare services (such as maternal health, paediatrics, and emergency care) that are crucial for addressing specific health needs in the population. Identifying operational facilities and enhancing their capabilities is vital to meet these needs.

  • Facility Conditions: Even operational facilities may face challenges related to infrastructure quality, including inadequate utilities (electricity, water, sanitation). Assessing the condition of these facilities is essential to ensure they can effectively deliver healthcare services.

How our map be used by decision-makers to improve infrastructure planning

  • Visualising Current Infrastructure: The map provides a visual representation of healthcare facilities, including their operational status. This allows decision-makers to quickly identify areas with a high concentration of facilities as well as regions that may be underserved or lacking adequate healthcare infrastructure.

  • Identifying Gaps and Needs: By highlighting the facilities with an "unknown operational" status, decision-makers can prioritise areas for investigation and assessment. This information can help identify gaps in healthcare services, allowing for targeted interventions in regions that may need more operational facilities or improved services.

  • Resource Allocation: The map can inform decisions on where to allocate resources, funding, and support. Understanding which areas lack operational facilities can guide investments in infrastructure, training, and equipment to ensure that healthcare services are accessible to all communities.

  • Supporting Data-Driven Decision-Making: The map integrates data from the Nigeria Health Facility Registry, providing a solid foundation for data-driven decision-making. Policymakers can rely on this data to support their proposals and funding requests, ensuring that they are addressing the actual needs of the population.

  • Enhancing Stakeholder Collaboration: The map can facilitate collaboration between various stakeholders, including government agencies, healthcare providers, and non-governmental organisations. By visualising the healthcare landscape, stakeholders can align their efforts and resources to address infrastructure needs more effectively.

  • Monitoring and Evaluation: The map can be used as a baseline for monitoring the progress of infrastructure development over time. Decision-makers can evaluate the impact of initiatives and policies by comparing updated maps to the original data, enabling them to assess which strategies are most effective in improving healthcare access and quality.

  • Community Engagement: The map can also be used to engage the community in discussions about healthcare needs and priorities. By sharing the map with community members, decision-makers can gather input on local challenges and opportunities, ensuring that planning efforts are aligned with the needs of the population.

  • Emergency Preparedness: In times of crisis, such as pandemics or natural disasters, the map can help decision-makers quickly identify operational facilities that can provide critical services, ensuring a coordinated response and resource distribution in affected areas.

Features of our map

  • Zoom Functionality: Users can easily zoom in and out of the map, enabling a closer look at specific areas of interest or a broader view of the overall infrastructure distribution.

  • Layered Infrastructure Data: The map supports multiple layers, allowing users to view different types of infrastructure data. Users can toggle between layers for healthcare (hospitals), education (schools), and utilities, ensuring that they can focus on the most relevant information based on their needs.

  • Interactive Points: Each infrastructure point on the map is clickable, revealing a popup with additional details. When users click on a specific facility, they will see:

    • Facility Name: The name of the infrastructure.

    • Type: The category of the infrastructure (e.g., hospital, school).

    • Status: The current operational status (e.g., functional, non-functional, unknown).

    • Additional information: Any other relevant details that enhance understanding (e.g., address, contact information).

  • Data Filtering: Users can filter the displayed data based on specific categories like hospitals, schools, or utilities. This feature is designed to simplify data exploration, allowing users to quickly identify and focus on the infrastructure most relevant to their interests or needs.

  • User-Friendly: The map is built with an intuitive interface that facilitates easy navigation. Users can quickly switch between different layers, zoom in for details, and access information with just a click.

  • Dynamic Updates: The map can be updated dynamically, allowing for the addition of new data points or modifications to existing ones. This ensures that the information presented is always current and relevant.

Conclusion

Data on healthcare infrastructure is critical for shaping the future of Nigeria. It lays the foundation for informed decision-making, promotes equitable access to healthcare, and supports the sustainable development of the nation, ultimately contributing to the well-being and prosperity of its citizens.
